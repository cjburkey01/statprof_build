Inverse Normal Cumulative Distribution Probability

From a normal distribution with mean μ and standard deviation σ, calculate which values a random sample might take with a given probability p. Calculates which values a sample has p likelihood of occurring below, above, or between.

This calculator can be used to generate a confidence interval. For example, to calculate the bounds of a 95% confidence interval, input 0.95 as p (and provide the mean and standard deviation, if needed), and observe the "x inside" output values for the lower and upper bounds.

If you need a z-score output, keep the mean μ at 0 and standard deviation σ at 1.
